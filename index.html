Green AI: Balancing Detection Accuracy and Energy Consumption in Android Malware Classification.
Shrinidhi Sridhar, Dr. Vikas Malviya
In progress — MIE-SPPU, 2026
This study investigates the trade-off between detection performance and energy consumption in Android malware classification. Lightweight deep learning models of various flavours are evaluated on the Drebin and TUANDROMD datasets, with all classifiers deployed in TensorFlow Lite (TFLite) format to reflect real-world mobile constraints. A comprehensive evaluation framework using AI Benchmark Pro and Android Studio is employed to measure inference latency and energy consumption. The impact of INT8 quantization on both performance and efficiency is also analyzed. Through systematic experimentation and statistical analysis, the study characterizes accuracy–energy trade-offs and provides insights for designing energy-efficient malware detection systems for resource-constrained Android devices.

Calibration Collapse Under Distribution Shift: A Study of Predictive Reliability in Machine Learning Models.
Shrinidhi Sridhar
Research project — 2026
Machine learning models are typically evaluated using predictive accuracy; however, accuracy alone does not reflect how reliable a model’s confidence estimates are in real-world deployment. In this work, we investigate the behavior of model calibration and predictive uncertainty under controlled data degradation and distribution shift. We conduct a systematic empirical study across multiple model families, including linear models and neural networks of varying complexity, and evaluate their performance under clean and perturbed data conditions. Data degradation is introduced through controlled mechanisms such as input noise, feature corruption, label noise, and distribution shifts, allowing us to analyze how calibration metrics evolve as the data environment becomes increasingly challenging.
